Method. The base is liquid surplus cash only. Property and equipment are excluded, because the event that destroys them is often the same event you would need them to absorb. The line sits at cash × (1 − 1/e), the loss at which the felt cost reaches one natural unit (Bernoulli, 1738); above it, a loss cannot be carried without risking the business. Recovering any loss takes extra sales of L / gross margin, a floor that sits before tax and fixed costs. No probability and no time horizon are assumed.
