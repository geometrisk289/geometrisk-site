Why Risk Matters

Profit is finite. Risk is uncapped.

You keep a fixed slice of every sale. The loss you keep has no such limit — and no policy is coming for it.

The downside has no ceiling

Your profit is a fixed slice of each sale. A loss you keep is not — one bad day can undo years of them.

Insurance stops at the exclusions

Sub-limits, exclusions, and the risks no insurer will write. Whatever’s left sits on your balance sheet, usually unmeasured.

A loss costs more than the loss

To rebuild what you retain, you have to sell it many times over. Knowing that climb before it lands is the whole point.

How it works

Know what you can carry before the loss lands.

  1. 01 Your business Annual revenue and the cash you could reach in the event of a loss.
  2. 02 The worst loss Set the one-off hit you’d most fear in your world, and see whether it sits within your line or past it.
  3. 03 The climb back For a loss past the line, the extra sales it would take to recover — and how far that climb really stretches.
