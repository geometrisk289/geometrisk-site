How to read this
  • Illustrative only. Northbridge Joinery, the broker, the policies and the premiums are fictional. This shows how the workbench records decisions, not a real placement.
  • Decisions are judged against the line, not the last renewal. "Cheaper" only counts as a win where the risk given up was absorbable; "dearer" is right where it closes a tail.
  • Retention is placed at or under the absorbable line. Here the largest single-event retention is $50k against a $95k line — headroom left on purpose.
  • Spend is reallocated, not stripped. The frequency layer funds the tail; the total falls because the bottom layer is expensive per dollar and the tail is cheap.
  • The method behind the base and the curve is in the Risk Library; the before/after view is in the Risk Optimisation Report.
